Chronological Journey

Key Milestones in the Seerah

570 CE — Year of the Elephant

Birth of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

Born in Makkah to Aminah bint Wahb and Abdullah ibn Abdul Muttalib. His father had passed away before his birth. He was cared for by his grandfather Abdul Muttalib and later wet-nursed by Halimah as-Sa'diyah.

610 CE — Age 40

First Revelation — Hira Cave

The first verses of the Quran were revealed in the Cave of Hira through Jibril (AS): "Iqra bismi rabbika alladhi khalaq..." The beginning of prophethood and the mission of Islam.

615 CE

Migration to Abyssinia

Due to intense persecution by the Quraysh, the Prophet ﷺ advised a group of Muslims to migrate to Abyssinia (Ethiopia) under the protection of the just King Ashama an-Najashi.

619 CE — Year of Sorrow

Passing of Khadijah RA and Abu Talib

The Prophet ﷺ lost his beloved wife Khadijah RA and his uncle and protector Abu Talib in the same year — a year of immense personal and communal grief for the early Muslims.

620 CE

Isra wal Miraj — The Night Journey

The miraculous night journey from Makkah to Jerusalem (Al-Aqsa) and then through the heavens, where the Prophet ﷺ met previous Prophets and received the gift of five daily prayers.

622 CE — Hijri Calendar Begins

Hijrah to Madinah

The migration of the Prophet ﷺ from Makkah to Madinah, marking the beginning of the Islamic calendar. He established the first Islamic state, built Masjid an-Nabawi, and united the Ansar and Muhajireen.

624 CE

Battle of Badr

The first major battle of Islam — 313 Muslims faced an army of 1,000 Quraysh fighters. Allah granted a decisive victory to the believers. This battle is called Yawm al-Furqan (the Day of Criterion).

630 CE — 8 AH

Conquest of Makkah — Fath Makkah

The Prophet ﷺ entered Makkah with 10,000 Muslims in a peaceful conquest. He declared general amnesty and purified the Kaabah of idols. "This is the day of mercy, not of war."

632 CE — 10 AH

Hajjat al-Wida — The Farewell Pilgrimage

The Prophet ﷺ delivered his final sermon at Arafat to over 100,000 Muslims. He declared the completion of religion: "Today I have perfected for you your religion..." He passed away in Madinah shortly after.
