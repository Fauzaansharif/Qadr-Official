About the Course

Learning the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ

Hadith Studies is the discipline of learning the sayings (Qawl), actions (Fi'l), and silent approvals (Taqrir) of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. As the second primary source of Islamic law after the Quran, Hadith guides every aspect of a Muslim's life — worship, character, family, and society.

Our course begins with accessible collections like Arba'een An-Nawawi and progresses to the major Kutub as-Sittah (Six Books of Hadith), ensuring students can understand, apply, and appreciate the blessed Sunnah.

⏱ Duration: 6–12 months
📊 Level: Intermediate
🗣 Language: Urdu & English

Syllabus Overview

  • Introduction to Sciences of Hadith (Mustalah al-Hadith)
  • Arba'een An-Nawawi — 42 essential Hadith
  • Riyad as-Salihin — Gardens of the Righteous
  • Bulugh al-Maram — Hadith of legal rulings
  • Selected Hadith from Sahih al-Bukhari
  • Selected Hadith from Sahih Muslim
  • Classification of Hadith: Sahih, Hasan, Da'if
  • Understanding Isnad (chain of narrators)

Major Collections

Kutub as-Sittah — The Six Books

The six most authentic collections of Hadith that form the cornerstone of Islamic scholarship

📗

Sahih al-Bukhari

Compiled by Imam Muhammad ibn Ismail al-Bukhari. Considered the most authentic book after the Quran. Contains 7,275 Hadith.

📘

Sahih Muslim

Compiled by Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj. Contains approximately 7,500 Hadith. Regarded as the second most authentic collection.

📙

Sunan Abu Dawud

Compiled by Imam Abu Dawud as-Sijistani. Focuses heavily on jurisprudential Hadith. Contains around 5,274 Hadith.

📕

Jami' at-Tirmidhi

Compiled by Imam at-Tirmidhi. Unique for including notes on the authenticity and application of each narration.

📓

Sunan an-Nasa'i

Compiled by Imam Ahmad ibn Shu'ayb an-Nasa'i. Known for its stringent criteria in narrator evaluation.

📒

Sunan Ibn Majah

Compiled by Imam Ibn Majah al-Qazwini. Widely used in Islamic jurisprudence, containing over 4,300 narrations.
